# SU(2) Curvature Scaling Limit

This layer is the non-Abelian companion to the U(1) curvature scaling test.

The experiment samples a smooth synthetic SU(2) connection on increasingly fine
square lattices.  The connection is represented as Hermitian Pauli-vector fields

\[
A_x(x,y)=a_x(x,y)\cdot\sigma,
\qquad
A_y(x,y)=a_y(x,y)\cdot\sigma.
\]

Links are generated by midpoint line-integral samples,

\[
U_{ij}=\exp\left(i\,((p_j-p_i)_x A_x(m_{ij})+(p_j-p_i)_y A_y(m_{ij}))\right).
\]

With this convention, the analytic non-Abelian curvature vector is

\[
F_{xy}=\partial_x a_y-\partial_y a_x-2a_x\times a_y.
\]

For a small plaquette of side length \(h\), the ordinary plaquette holonomy
satisfies, in the small-area limit,

\[
W_\square \approx \exp(i F_{xy}h^2).
\]

The experiment computes the principal SU(2) logarithm vector of each plaquette holonomy and compares

\[
\frac{\log(W_\square)}{h^2}
\]

against the analytic curvature vector at the plaquette center.  In the non-Abelian case, this vector comparison has a basepoint subtlety: the plaquette logarithm is based at a corner of the square, while the analytic curvature is sampled at the center. Without explicitly parallel-transporting the center curvature into the basepoint fiber, the raw vector comparison converges roughly first order. Conjugacy-invariant norm diagnostics recover the expected second-order midpoint scaling.

The Her-specific piece is the reconstruction check: the ordinary plaquette
holonomy is reconstructed from Her triangle residuals using the identity already
established in the plaquette/holonomy layer.  The reconstruction error remains
near machine precision.

## Established Statement

In a smooth synthetic SU(2) connection, Her-reconstructed plaquette holonomy follows the expected non-Abelian curvature-times-area behavior under lattice refinement. The raw Lie-algebra vector comparison converges, and the conjugacy-invariant norm diagnostic shows near-second-order scaling. The result is stronger than the U(1) scaling test because the analytic target includes the commutator term.

## Boundary

This still does not prove that a Her residual is literally curvature in every
setting.  It shows that Her residuals can reconstruct standard non-Abelian
plaquette holonomy, and that this reconstructed holonomy has the expected
small-area curvature scaling in a controlled SU(2) test.
